Political Career

74 BC

Caesar was made a member of the politico-ecclesiastical college of pontifices; and on his return he gained one of the elective military tribuneships.

69 BC- 65 BC

Quaestor is the lowest-ranking regular magistrate in ancient Rome, whose traditional responsibility was the treasury. Caesar served his quaestorship in the province of Farther Spain

65 BC - 63 BC

Aedile is the magistrate responsible for the care and supervision of the markets, also issued edicts.

63 BC

Pontifex maximus was the chief high priest of the College of Pontiffs in ancient Rome. This was the most important position in the ancient Roman religion.

62 BC

Praetor is a judicial officer who had broad authority in cases of equity, was responsible for the production of the public games, and, in the absence of consuls, exercised extensive authority in the government.

60 BC

The First Triumvirate was an informal political alliance among three prominent politicians in the late Roman Republic: Gaius Julius Caesar, Gnaeus Pompeius Magnus and Marcus Licinius Crassus. The constitution of the Roman republic had many veto points. In order to bypass constitutional obstacles and force through the political goals of the three men

59 BC

Consul in ancient Rome, either of the two highest of the ordinary magistracies in the ancient Roman Republic.

46 BC - 44 BC

Dictator which in the Roman Republic designated a temporary magistrate who was granted extraordinary powers in order to deal with state crises.

44 BC - 15 march

The conspiracy to assassinate Julius Caesar began with a meeting between Cassius Longinus and his brother-in-law Marcus Brutus in the evening of 22 February 44 BC, when after some discussion the two agreed that something had to be done to prevent Caesar from becoming king of the Romans. He was assassinated by a group of nobles in the Senate House on the Ides of March.
